DELTA-Hub is hosting a two-day practical workshop on climate change in deltaic, coastal, and marine environments, with a central focus on the Danube Delta and the Black Sea.

Participants will learn how to access global open datasets (Copernicus CDS, satellite imagery, climate reanalyses), use Python for environmental data analysis, work effectively with AI coding assistants, and apply basic statistical methods. The programme features an invited lecture by Prof. Edward Anthony (CEREGE, Aix-Marseille Université) — *How River Deltas Work: Physical Processes and the Forces that Shape Them*.

You can bring your own dataset or research question, provided it relates to deltaic, coastal, marine, or fluvial environments.

**Programme**

- Friday 22 May — training modules and guided work on data
- Saturday 23 May (morning) — project work
- Saturday (afternoon) — 3-minute project pitches

Two €100 prizes will be awarded for the best results, and selected projects will be published on the DELTA-Hub website. Lunch is provided on both days.

**Who can apply**

Open to students from any related field — including geography, geology, environmental sciences, oceanography, engineering, and informatics — who are able to attend in person at the Faculty of Geography in Bucharest. No prior coding experience required; just bring your laptop.

**How to apply**

20 places, allocated by selection. Send a 5-line email to [delta.hub@geo.unibuc.ro](mailto:delta.hub@geo.unibuc.ro) describing your relevant experience and why you'd like to take part.

**Deadline: 15 May 2026**

More information at [delta-hub.unibuc.ro](https://delta-hub.unibuc.ro/)
